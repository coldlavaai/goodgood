## What Actually Is CBD?

Let's start with the basics.

**CBD (cannabidiol)** is one of over 100 compounds found in cannabis and hemp plants. Unlike THC (tetrahydrocannabinol), CBD is:
- **Non-psychoactive** (it won't get you high)
- **Completely legal in the UK** (when it contains <0.2% THC)
- **Not addictive**
- **Extensively researched** for therapeutic benefits

When we talk about "CBD for skin," we're specifically referring to **topical CBD** - products you apply directly to your skin, not ingest.

## How Does CBD Work on Your Skin?

This is where it gets interesting.

Your skin has its own **endocannabinoid system (ECS)** - a complex network of receptors that helps regulate:
- Inflammation responses
- Cell growth and death
- Immune function
- Oil (sebum) production
- Pain and itch sensations

**Here's the science:** CBD interacts with these receptors (particularly CB1 and CB2 receptors) to help modulate these processes. Think of CBD as a "regulator" that helps bring your skin back into balance.

### What Does the Research Say?

Unlike many skincare trends that are all hype and no science, CBD has serious research backing it:

**Anti-inflammatory properties:**
- A 2019 study in *Clinical Therapeutics* found CBD significantly reduced inflammation in skin cells
- Research from King's College London showed CBD can suppress inflammatory cytokines (the chemicals that cause redness and swelling)

**Skin barrier repair:**

**Anti-itch effects:**

**Antioxidant benefits:**

- Helps protect skin from environmental damage and premature aging

## Is CBD Actually Legal in the UK?

**Yes - but with important caveats.**

In the UK, CBD products are legal if they meet these criteria:

1. **Derived from EU-approved hemp strains** (not marijuana)
2. **Contain less than 0.2% THC** (the legal limit)
3. **Sold as a "cosmetic product"** not a medicine (unless licensed by the MHRA)
4. **Properly labeled** with ingredients and safety information

**What this means for you:** Any reputable UK CBD skincare brand should provide:
- Third-party lab test results (showing THC content)
- Clear ingredient lists
- Batch numbers for traceability
- Compliance with UK cosmetics regulations

If a brand can't provide these, walk away.

## CBD Concentration: How Much Do You Actually Need?

This is where most people get confused - and where many brands mislead you.

**The harsh truth:** Many CBD skincare products contain such tiny amounts of CBD that they're essentially useless. A moisturizer with "5mg CBD" is marketing, not medicine.

**What you need:**

For mild skin dryness: **500-1000mg CBD per 50ml**
For moderate eczema/psoriasis: **1500-2000mg CBD per 50ml**
For severe or resistant conditions: **2400mg+ CBD per 50ml**

**Bonus ingredient:** Look for products that also contain **CBG (cannabigerol)**. Research suggests CBG works synergistically with CBD and has its own anti-inflammatory properties. The ideal ratio is about 4:1 (CBD:CBG).

## How to Use CBD Skincare Effectively

Getting the most out of your CBD products requires proper application:

**Best practices:**

1. **Apply to clean, slightly damp skin** (within 3 minutes of washing)
2. **Use less than you think** - CBD balms are concentrated; a little goes a long way
3. **Massage in thoroughly** - helps absorption
4. **Be consistent** - CBD builds up in your skin over time; use daily for best results
5. **Give it time** - Unlike steroid creams, natural CBD takes 1-3 weeks to show full effects

**Pro tip:** For eczema flare-ups, apply a thin layer 2-3 times daily. For maintenance, once daily (usually at night) is sufficient.

## FAQs

**Will CBD skincare show up on a drug test?**
No. Topical CBD products contain negligible THC (<0.2%) and are applied externally. They won't enter your bloodstream in amounts that would trigger a drug test.

**Can I use CBD skincare if I'm pregnant or breastfeeding?**
Current guidance is to avoid CBD during pregnancy/breastfeeding due to insufficient research. Always consult your midwife or GP.

**Does CBD skincare smell like cannabis?**
High-quality CBD extracted properly should have minimal to no cannabis smell. Look for products with natural essential oils if scent is important to you.

**How should I store CBD skincare?**
Keep in a cool, dark place. CBD can degrade with heat and light exposure. Many products last 12-18 months if stored properly.

**Can children use CBD skincare?**
Topical CBD is generally considered safe for children over 6 months, but always consult your pediatrician first, especially for medical conditions.
